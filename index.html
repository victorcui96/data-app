What are internet sessions?

An internet session is a time frame of activity that a user (sometimes called a client) with a unique IP address spends on a website, which resides on a server. Internet sessions are known to have a positive correlation with internet usage. The more internet sessions a certain area has, generally the higher the amount of people in that area using the internet

Internet Facts

1983

TCP/IP, a new communications protocol governing computer networks, is born. TCP/IP allowed different kinds of computers on different networks to "talk" to each other. TCP/IP forms the backbone of the internet. It's one of the reasons why you are viewing this site!

Cookies

A Cookie is a special text sent by servers and kept by clients. They work together with sessions to provide custom, interactive user experiences. Together, they are what makes your facebook feed different from your buddy's!

Gotta Have my Wi-fi

Since it was introduced in 1985 and especially during the last two decades, Wireless Internet, or "wi-fi", has exploded in popularity. Wi-fi currently carries more than 60% of the world's internet traffic. So how do my bits get transferred to the internet without a physical wire, you ask? Well, wi-fi essentially works throgh radio waves.

Wi-fi depends on two radios communicating back and forth that use lower power and broadcast over a much shorter distance. Wi-fi depends on the IP protocol to communicate and transfer data over the radio waves. IP is the language of ther internet, making wi-fi very resilient and stuctured.
