What are internet sessions?

An internet session is a time frame of activity that a user (sometimes called a client) with a unique IP address spends on a website, which resides on a server. Internet sessions are known to have a positive correlation with internet usage. Generally, the more internet sessions a certain area has, the higher the amount of people in that area using the internet

Internet Facts

1983

TCP/IP, a new communications protocol governing computer networks, is born. TCP/IP allowed different kinds of computers on different networks to "talk" to each other. TCP/IP forms the backbone of the internet. It's one of the reasons why you are able to view this site!

Cookies

A Cookie is a special kind of text sent by servers and kept by clients. They work together with sessions to provide custom, interactive user experiences. Together, they are what makes your facebook feed different from your buddy's!

Gotta Have my Wi-fi

Since it was introduced in 1985 and especially during the last two decades, Wireless Internet, or "wi-fi", has exploded in popularity. Wi-fi currently carries more than 60% of the world's internet traffic. So how does wifi transmit computer data without a physical wire? Well, it essentially works through radio waves.

Wi-fi depends on two radios communicating back and forth that use lower power and broadcast over a much shorter distance. Wi-fi uses the IP protocol to communicate and transfer data over the radio waves. IP is the language of the internet, making wi-fi very resilient and stuctured.
