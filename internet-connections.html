Types of Internet Connections

There are many different ways to connect to the internet, including Wireless, Mobile cell Towers, Dial-up, DSL, Cable, Satellite, and Broadband. They all have varying costs, speed, availability, and bandwidth (the maximum transfer rate of the network).

I will present data relating to Broadband internet connections, which are high-speed connections provided through either cable or telephone companies. Broadband connections are considered high-bandwidth because they use multiple data channels to send and receive internet data.

Internet of Things

In early 2014, the concept of "Internet of Things (IoT)" reached mass market and into consumers' daily lingo. IoT are sensors and actuators embedded in physical objects linked through wired and wireless networks, connecting them to the internet.

With the rise of IoT, gaming consoles, Web cams, automobiles, and even toasters are being hooked up to the Internet.

Explanation of DSL, Cable, and Fibre Optic

  • DSL— uses existing 2-wire copper telephone line connection to your house so phone companies deliver internet and landline telephone service at the same time. Speed = 1.5 Mbps - 100 Mbps
  • Cellular, Satellite, Cable, Wireless— Various internet connection technologies. Speed = 1.5 Mbps for Cellular, Satellite, and Wireless, 10 Mbps - 120 Mpbs for Cable.
  • Fibre Optic— Uses a light signal and a type of flexible glass as the medium. Fast, but expensive and complicated to install. Speed = ~100 Mbps, up to 1 Gbps
