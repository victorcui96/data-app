Types of Internet Connections

There are many different ways to connect to the internet, including Wireless, Mobile cell Towers, Dial-up, DSL, Cable, Satellite, and Broadband. They all have varying costs, speed, availability, and bandwidth (the maximum transfer rate of the network).

I will present data relating to Broadband internet connections, which are high-speed connections provided through either cable or telephone companies. Broadband connections are considered high-bandwidth because they use multiple data channels to send and receive internet data.

Explanation of DSL, Cable, and Fibre Optic

    DSL

    uses existing 2-wire copper telephone line connection to your house

    Speed = 1.5 Mbps - 100 Mbps

    Cellular, Satellite, Cable, Wireless

    Various internet connection technologies.

    Speed = 1.5 Mbps for Cellular, Satellite, and Wireless

    Speed = 10 Mbps - 120 Mpbs for Cable.

    Fibre Optic

    Uses a light signal and a type of flexible glass as the medium.

    Fast, but expensive and complicated to install.

    Speed = ~100 Mbps, up to 1 Gbps
